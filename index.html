The Engagement

On a seemingly ordinary Saturday in July 2017, Christie and Will visited the Minnesota Science Museum to make use of a gifted membership. It was nice to be in the AC on such a hot day. Will asked Christie to go get tickets since he had to call his new job about something. This was actually him calling her parents to tell them he was going to propose. Christie didn’t question his need to call a public school district on a Saturday during the summer.

After the museum, it was nearly sunset and Christie was starving. She suggested getting dinner instead of taking a walk as originally planned. Will, wanting to preserve his secret plan, said, “The lighting’s really nice right now. Let’s just go to Irvine Park really quick and walk around, then we can decide what to eat.” Christie, amazingly, was immediately convinced, and suspected nothing.

What is usually a deserted yet beautiful park a few streets of the beaten path from downtown Saint Paul was, for the first time ever, filled with people. Not wanting a grand, public display, Will now realized he had to stall a bit until the park cleared out.

They sat on the last open bench and chatted and tried to decide where to eat. Will took this time to pretend to forget the name of a nearby restaurant which he knew was closed. Slowly the park began to clear. They decided on tacos.

Christie started walking away and Will stopped and kissed her. Christie replied romantically with, “Okay, let’s go, I’m really hungry.”
Will got down on one knee, said the words, and forgot which hand to put the ring on.
She said yes. They got tacos.
